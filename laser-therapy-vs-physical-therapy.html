● How They Differ

How Laser Therapy Works

Laser therapy uses focused light energy to penetrate tissue, stimulate cellular repair, reduce inflammation, and accelerate healing. Sessions take 5 to 10 minutes. You feel warmth but no pain. There's no exercise involved. The laser does the work at a cellular level.

● How They Differ

How Physical Therapy Works

Physical therapy uses exercise, stretching, manual techniques, and movement training to restore function, build strength, and improve mobility. Sessions last 30 to 60 minutes and require active patient participation. PT addresses the biomechanical problems that contributed to the injury.

● Choose Laser

When Laser Is the Better Starting Point

Laser therapy is the better first step when inflammation or tissue damage needs to be addressed before exercise can begin.

  • Acute inflammation that's too painful for exercise
  • Soft tissue damage from an auto accident or injury
  • Neuropathy or nerve pain
  • Conditions where the tissue needs to heal before strengthening can begin
  • Post-surgical inflammation
● Choose PT

When PT Is the Better Starting Point

Physical therapy is the right first step when strength, stability, and movement patterns need to be rebuilt.

  • Chronic weakness or instability
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation requiring structured exercise
  • Balance and coordination problems
  • Return-to-sport programming
  • Movement pattern retraining
● Better Together

Why They Work Best Together

Laser therapy reduces inflammation and pain so patients can participate in rehabilitation exercises more effectively. PT then builds the strength and stability needed to prevent the injury from recurring.
