What Types of Therapy Treatment Are Available for Pain Relief?

Most pain involves more than one tissue type. A disc problem irritates a nerve. That nerve causes a muscle to spasm. That spasm pulls a joint out of alignment. Treating just one piece leaves the others to keep the cycle going.

At Novaré™, we build therapy treatment plans using multiple modalities that target different layers of the problem. Here's what's available at our Fort Myers and Lehigh Acres clinics:

  • Class IV Laser Therapy: High-powered medical lasers that penetrate deep into tissue, stimulating cellular repair and reducing inflammation at the source. Our AI-guided laser system adjusts treatment parameters in real time based on tissue response.
  • Manual Therapy: Hands-on techniques including myofascial release, joint mobilization, trigger point therapy, and soft tissue work performed by our licensed massage therapists, Santeria Gadsden and Helen Leal.
  • Shockwave Therapy: Acoustic pressure waves delivered by our Intelect RPW 2 system to break up scar tissue, calcifications, and chronic adhesions that haven't responded to other treatments.
  • Rehabilitative Exercises: Targeted movements and strengthening protocols that support what we correct in the clinic, so results hold between visits.
  • Spinal Decompression: Motorized traction that creates space between vertebrae, reducing pressure on compressed discs and nerves using $25,000 flexion-distraction tables.

Each modality does something the others can't. The combination is what makes the difference.

● Therapy vs Chiropractic

When Is Therapy Enough on Its Own vs. When Do You Need Chiropractic Too?

Some conditions respond to therapy alone. Muscle strains, mild tendinitis, post-surgical stiffness, and soft tissue inflammation often improve with a combination of laser, manual therapy, and rehab exercises without any spinal adjustments.

But many pain conditions involve a structural component. A misaligned vertebra compresses a nerve. A locked facet joint creates muscle guarding. In those cases, therapy treats the symptoms while chiropractic care addresses the root cause. You need both.

Here's how Dr. Bracic determines the right approach:

  • Therapy alone: Soft tissue injuries without joint dysfunction, post-surgical recovery, muscle-only conditions, peripheral neuropathy, plantar fasciitis
  • Chiropractic + therapy: Disc herniations, sciatica with spinal involvement, whiplash, joint misalignment causing nerve compression, chronic conditions with both structural and muscular components
  • Therapy as maintenance: Patients who've completed chiropractic correction and need ongoing soft tissue support to maintain results

The first visit ($200) includes a full evaluation where Dr. Bracic examines your spine, joints, and soft tissue to determine which therapy combination will get you the fastest results.

What Conditions Respond to Non-Surgical Therapy?

Our therapy modalities treat a wide range of pain and injury conditions.

Back and Neck Pain

Muscle tension, disc bulges, facet joint irritation, and postural strain all respond to therapy. We often combine spinal decompression with manual therapy and laser for multi-layer spinal conditions.

Neuropathy

Peripheral neuropathy causes numbness, tingling, and burning in the hands and feet. Low level laser therapy treatment stimulates nerve cell regeneration and increases blood flow to damaged nerves. Patients often report feeling returning after a series of sessions.

Plantar Fasciitis

Shockwave therapy breaks up chronic inflammation in the plantar fascia while laser accelerates tissue healing. This combination often works when cortisone shots and orthotics haven't.

Accident Injuries

Auto accidents, slip-and-fall injuries, and workplace incidents create multiple tissue injuries at once. Our accident care program uses every therapy modality to treat the full scope of the injury, not just the loudest symptom.

Sciatica

Sciatic nerve pain radiating down the leg can come from disc compression, piriformis syndrome, or spinal stenosis. Decompression addresses disc-related sciatica. Manual therapy and laser treat soft tissue and nerve inflammation directly.
