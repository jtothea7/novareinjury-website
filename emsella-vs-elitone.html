● Emsella Chair

What Is the Emsella Chair?

The Emsella is an FDA-cleared, clinical-grade device that uses high-intensity focused electromagnetic energy (HIFEM) to deliver approximately 11,000 supramaximal pelvic floor contractions per 30-minute session. You sit fully clothed on the chair. The contractions are deep and powerful enough to target both slow-twitch and fast-twitch muscle fibers.

The standard protocol is 6 sessions over 3 weeks. Emsella is available only through licensed healthcare providers.

● Elitone

What Is Elitone?

Elitone is an FDA-cleared, at-home device that uses electrical muscle stimulation (EMS) to contract the pelvic floor muscles externally. It's a small pad worn under clothing for 20-minute sessions. You can use it at home without a provider visit.

Elitone is designed for mild stress incontinence and is available for consumer purchase.

Choose Emsella If...

The Emsella Chair is the stronger choice when you need clinical-level results.

  • Your incontinence is moderate to severe
  • You want faster, clinically supervised results
  • You've tried at-home devices or Kegels without improvement
  • You want deep muscle engagement that surface-level devices can't reach
  • You prefer a short treatment course (3 weeks) over daily device use
● Choose Elitone

Choose Elitone If...

Elitone may be a reasonable starting point for milder symptoms.

  • Your incontinence is mild
  • You want to start with a lower-cost option
  • You prefer treating at home on your own schedule
  • You're comfortable with a longer treatment timeline
  • Your symptoms don't significantly impact your daily life
● The Bottom Line

The Honest Bottom Line

Elitone is a reasonable starting point for mild incontinence. It's affordable and convenient. But if Elitone or other at-home devices haven't given you the improvement you want, the Emsella Chair delivers significantly deeper muscle stimulation at a clinical level that consumer devices can't match.
