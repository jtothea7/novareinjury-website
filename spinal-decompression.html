What Is Spinal Decompression and How Does It Work?

Spinal decompression is a non-surgical treatment that gently stretches the spine using computer-controlled traction. The stretching creates negative pressure inside the spinal discs, which helps retract bulging or herniated disc material, takes pressure off compressed nerves, and draws nutrients back into damaged discs to promote healing.

Each session lasts 30 to 45 minutes. You lie on a motorized table while the system cycles through stretching and relaxation phases. Most patients describe it as a pulling sensation, not painful. A typical plan runs 20 to 30 sessions over 6 to 8 weeks.

  • Herniated or bulging discs
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Sciatica and nerve compression
  • Spinal stenosis narrowing the canal
  • Failed back surgery syndrome
  • Chronic lower back pain
  • MRI required before treatment

Chiropractic Techniques at Novaré

Three distinct approaches, selected based on your diagnosis and comfort level.

1

Cox Flexion Distraction

A gentle, non-surgical technique performed on a specially designed table that provides controlled stretching and decompression of the spine. It increases spinal mobility, reduces pressure on discs and nerves, and treats herniated discs, sciatica, and spinal stenosis. The table adjusts to accommodate patients experiencing pain, ensuring a comfortable treatment experience. Most plans involve multiple sessions for best results.

2

Pro Adjuster SRT

An instrument-based chiropractic tool that uses repetitive pulsing at specific frequencies instead of manual cracking. The pulsing gradually increases in intensity, stimulating mechanical receptors in joints and muscles to promote healing and relaxation. Good for patients who prefer a gentler approach or who are uncomfortable with traditional manual adjustments.

3

Manual Spinal Adjustments

Traditional hands-on adjustments to restore alignment, reduce nerve pressure, and improve joint mobility. Dr. Bracic has performed spinal adjustments for over 30 years and adapts his technique to each patient's condition, age, and comfort level.
